# Data Processing Agreement

**BarangayOS — Barangay Records Management System**

*Template Version 1.0 — July 2026*

---

## Preamble

This Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") is entered into by and between:

**The Data Controller** — the Barangay Local Government Unit (LGU) that deploys and operates the BarangayOS system ("Controller")

**and**

**The Data Processor** — the entity or individual that processes personal data on behalf of the Controller using the BarangayOS system ("Processor")

*This DPA supplements the underlying agreement between the Controller and the Processor and is governed by Republic Act No. 10173 (Data Privacy Act of 2012) and its implementing rules and regulations ("Data Privacy Laws").*

---

## 1. Definitions

1.1 **"Data Privacy Laws"** means Republic Act No. 10173 (Data Privacy Act of 2012) of the Philippines, its Implementing Rules and Regulations, and all related issuances of the National Privacy Commission (NPC).

1.2 **"Personal Data"** means any information, whether recorded in a material form or not, from which the identity of an individual is apparent or can be reasonably and directly ascertained by the entity holding the information, or when put together with other information would directly and certainly identify an individual.

1.3 **"Processing"** means any operation or set of operations performed upon Personal Data including, but not limited to, collection, recording, organization, storage, updating or modification, retrieval, consultation, use, consolidation, blocking, erasure or destruction of data.

1.4 **"Data Subject"** means an individual whose Personal Data is processed.

1.5 **"Data Breach"** means a breach of security leading to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to, Personal Data transmitted, stored, or otherwise processed.

1.6 **"Sub-processor"** means a third party engaged by the Processor to process Personal Data on behalf of the Controller, with the Controller's prior written authorization.

1.7 **"BarangayOS"** means the open-source Barangay Records Management System software, including its frontend application, PocketBase backend, and associated components.

---

## 2. Scope and Purpose

2.1 This DPA sets out the terms under which the Processor may process Personal Data on behalf of the Controller through the use of BarangayOS.

2.2 The DPA applies to all Processing of Personal Data within BarangayOS, including but not limited to:
   - Resident profile data
   - Document request data
   - Blotter and complaint records
   - Visitor log data
   - Household data
   - Financial records containing personal information of transacting parties
   - Activity and audit logs
   - Any other Personal Data entered into or generated by the system

---

## 3. Description of Processing

### 3.1 Categories of Data Subjects

- Residents of the Barangay
- Document applicants and requestors
- Complainants and respondents in blotter/complaint records
- Visitors to the Barangay Hall
- Household members
- Barangay staff, officials, and employees (to the extent their data appears in the system)
- Transacting parties in financial records

### 3.2 Categories of Personal Data

**Standard Personal Data:**
- Full name, birth date, age, gender
- Contact number
- Address, purok/zone assignment
- Civil status, occupation
- Nationality
- Household affiliation
- Government identification references

**Sensitive Personal Data:**
- Blood type (medical data)
- Disability/PWD status
- 4Ps beneficiary status (socioeconomic indicator)
- Voter registration status
- Incident narratives in blotter records (may contain sensitive contextual information)
- Financial transaction details with personal identifiers

### 3.3 Nature and Purpose of Processing

The Processor processes Personal Data solely for the following purposes on behalf of the Controller:

- Maintaining the official Barangay registry of residents
- Processing document requests and issuing official documents
- Recording, investigating, and resolving complaints and disputes
- Visitor management and security monitoring
- Barangay financial management and record-keeping
- Statutory compliance and reporting (DILG, COA, DSWD, etc.)
- Archiving and historical record-keeping
- System auditing and activity monitoring

### 3.4 Processing Duration

Processing continues for the duration of the Controller's use of BarangayOS, plus any applicable statutory retention periods thereafter.

## 4. Processor Obligations

The Processor agrees to:

4.1 **Process only on documented instructions.** Personal Data shall only be processed on the documented instructions of the Controller, unless required to do so by applicable law. The Processor shall inform the Controller if, in its opinion, an instruction infringes Data Privacy Laws.

4.2 **Maintain confidentiality.** The Processor shall ensure that all personnel authorized to process Personal Data are bound by appropriate confidentiality obligations.

4.3 **Implement security measures.** The Processor shall implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect Personal Data, including those described in Section 7 of this DPA.

4.4 **Not engage sub-processors without authorization.** The Processor shall not engage another processor (sub-processor) without prior specific or general written authorization from the Controller.

4.5 **Assist the Controller.** The Processor shall assist the Controller in:
   - Responding to data subject requests (access, correction, erasure, objection, portability)
   - Complying with Data Privacy Law obligations regarding security, breach notification, and data protection impact assessments
   - Ensuring the security of processing

4.6 **Notify of data breaches.** The Processor shall notify the Controller without undue delay upon becoming aware of a Data Breach involving Personal Data processed under this DPA.

4.7 **Return or delete data.** At the Controller's election, the Processor shall delete or return all Personal Data at the end of the processing services, unless retention is required by law.

4.8 **Cease processing upon instructions.** The Processor shall immediately cease processing Personal Data upon the Controller's reasonable request or upon the Controller's determination that the Processor has violated any provision of this DPA.

## 5. Controller Obligations

The Controller agrees to:

5.1 Retain control over and responsibility for the Personal Data processed.

5.2 Ensure that it has a lawful basis for each Processing activity (consent, contractual necessity, legal obligation, public function, or legitimate interest as defined in RA 10173).

5.3 Provide Data Subjects with the required Privacy Notice (see `PRIVACY_NOTICE.md`).

5.4 Respond to Data Subject requests and complaints.

5.5 Ensure instructions to the Processor comply with Data Privacy Laws.

5.6 Conduct or require a Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) where Processing is likely to result in high risk to Data Subjects.

## 7. Technical and Organizational Security Measures

The Processor shall maintain the following minimum security measures:

### 7.1 Access Controls
- Role-based access control (admin, staff, viewer) with distinct permission sets
- Unique user accounts with email/password authentication
- Password hashing using bcrypt
- Session tokens with configurable expiry (default 5 days)
- Server-side authorization rule enforcement (cannot be bypassed by client-side manipulation)

### 7.2 Network Security
- HTTPS/TLS encryption for all data in transit
- Optional Cloudflare Web Application Firewall (WAF) protection
- Optional Cloudflare Tunnel for secure proxying without opening firewall ports

### 7.3 Data Storage Security
- SQLite database managed by PocketBase with optional encryption key (`PB_ENCRYPTION_KEY`)
- Session data encrypted at rest when encryption key is configured
- File storage with configurable mime-type restrictions (image uploads restricted to JPEG, PNG, SVG, GIF, WebP)

### 7.4 Audit and Monitoring
- Automatic activity logging for all create, update, and delete operations
- Separate finance audit log for all financial transactions
- Audit logs include user identity, action, timestamp, and record reference
- Logs are append-only (update of existing logs is not permitted by the system)

### 7.5 Offline Data Protection
- Pending changes cached in IndexedDB for offline resilience
- Data is synced automatically when connectivity is restored
- Offline queue is limited to the browser's local storage scope

### 7.6 Incident Response
- Breach notification to Controller within 72 hours
- Notification to NPC and affected Data Subjects as required by law

## 8. Data Breach Management

8.1 The Processor shall implement and maintain a Data Breach response plan.

8.2 In the event of a Data Breach, the Processor shall, without undue delay and no later than **24 hours** after becoming aware of the breach:
   - Notify the Controller providing:
     - A description of the nature of the breach
     - The categories and approximate number of Data Subjects and records concerned
     - The likely consequences of the breach
     - Measures taken or proposed to address the breach
   - Cooperate fully with the Controller in investigating and remediating the breach

8.3 The Controller shall be responsible for notifying the National Privacy Commission and affected Data Subjects in accordance with NPC Circular 16-03.

---

## 9. Data Subject Rights

9.1 The Processor shall assist the Controller in responding to Data Subject requests to exercise their rights under Data Privacy Laws, including:

| Right | Obligation |
|-------|------------|
| Right to be informed | Provide Privacy Notice to Data Subjects |
| Right to access | Facilitate access to Personal Data held in the system |
| Right to object | Flag records for restricted processing where applicable |
| Right to erasure | Delete or anonymize data, subject to legal retention requirements |
| Right to rectification | Correct inaccurate or incomplete data |
| Right to data portability | Export data in commonly used format (via BarangayOS export features) |
| Right to damages | Support the Controller in any claim assessment |

9.2 The Processor shall notify the Controller within **5 working days** of receiving any direct request from a Data Subject and shall not respond to the request without the Controller's prior authorization.

---

## 10. Data Retention and Deletion

10.1 The Processor shall retain Personal Data only for as long as necessary to fulfill the Processing purposes or as required by applicable law.

10.2 Upon termination of the agreement with the Controller, or upon the Controller's instruction, the Processor shall, at the Controller's option:
   - Delete all Personal Data from the system, or
   - Return all Personal Data in a commonly used format and subsequently delete it

10.3 Reference retention periods (subject to applicable law and Controller's policies):

| Data Category | Suggested Retention |
|---------------|-------------------|
| Resident profiles | Permanent (official registry) |
| Document requests | 5 years |
| Blotter records | 10 years from final disposition |
| Visitor logs | 1 year |
| Activity logs | 3 years |
| Finance records | 10 years |

10.4 Deletion shall be carried out using the system's built-in delete functions or PocketBase administrative tools, ensuring data is irrecoverable.

---

## 11. Audits and Inspections

11.1 The Controller may audit the Processor's compliance with this DPA, subject to reasonable notice (minimum 15 working days, except in cases of suspected breach or violation).

11.2 Audits shall be:
   - Conducted during regular business hours
   - At the Controller's expense
   - Limited in scope to Processing activities under this DPA
   - Conducted in a manner that does not disrupt the Processor's operations

11.3 The Processor shall provide reasonable access to:
   - System configurations and security settings
   - Activity and audit logs
   - Records of data Processing activities
   - Sub-processor agreements (redacted to protect third-party confidentiality)

---

## 12. International Data Transfers

12.1 By default, all Personal Data processed through BarangayOS is stored on the Controller's server within the Philippines.

12.2 If the Controller configures optional services (e.g., Cloudinary for image hosting), Personal Data may be transferred to servers outside the Philippines.

12.3 Any transfer of Personal Data to a country without adequate data protection standards shall be subject to appropriate safeguards, including:
   - Standard Contractual Clauses
   - Binding Corporate Rules
   - Consent of the Data Subject
   - Any other mechanism recognized under Data Privacy Laws

---

## 13. Liability and Indemnification

13.1 Each party shall be liable for damages arising from its own violation of this DPA or Data Privacy Laws.

13.2 The Processor shall indemnify the Controller against claims arising from the Processor's breach of this DPA, provided the Controller:
   - Promptly notifies the Processor of the claim
   - Provides reasonable cooperation in defending the claim
   - Does not admit fault or settle the claim without the Processor's prior written consent

13.3 The Controller shall indemnify the Processor against claims arising from the Controller's instructions that violate Data Privacy Laws or from the Controller's breach of its obligations under this DPA.

14. This DPA shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the Republic of the Philippines.

14.2 Any dispute arising out of or in connection with this DPA shall first be referred to mediation. If mediation fails, the dispute shall be submitted to the courts of the Philippines.

---

## 15. Term and Termination

15.1 This DPA shall take effect upon the Processor's first Processing of Personal Data on behalf of the Controller and shall continue until terminated.

15.2 Either party may terminate this DPA by giving **30 days'** written notice to the other party.

15.3 The Controller may terminate this DPA immediately if the Processor:
   - Breaches any material term of this DPA
   - Violates Data Privacy Laws in a manner that creates risk to Data Subjects
   - Becomes insolvent or is subject to insolvency proceedings

15.4 Upon termination, the Processor shall, at the Controller's direction, delete or return all Personal Data within **30 days**.

15.5 The obligations under Sections 4 (Processor Obligations), 8 (Data Breach Management), 9 (Data Subject Rights), 10 (Data Retention and Deletion), 11 (Audits), and 13 (Liability) shall survive termination of this DPA.
